# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads define quality standards, develop test strategies, and ensure that all delivered features meet acceptance criteria and quality benchmarks. They partner with developers and product teams to shift quality left and reduce defects in production.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Define quality metrics and acceptance thresholds
- Coordinate manual and automated testing efforts
- Identify quality risks and gaps early
- Lead acceptance testing and sign-off activities
- Report quality status in weekly syncs and demos

### Goals
- Maximize customer confidence through rigorous quality validation
- Reduce production defects and post-release firefighting
- Build fast, reliable feedback loops for developers

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning (quality review of acceptance criteria)
- Code review comments (testability and test coverage)
- Weekly quality reports and risk flagging
- Acceptance testing sign-off

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test automation, provide feedback on code testability
- **Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria, report on quality metrics
- **Project Managers**: Escalate quality risks and blockers, coordinate testing timelines

---

## Tech Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
Tech Leads guide technical direction, make architectural decisions, and mentor the engineering team on design patterns, scalability, and technical excellence. They balance delivery velocity with long-term system health.

### Responsibilities
- Design system architecture and technical approach
- Review and approve technical designs for complex features
- Mentor developers on coding standards and best practices
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Collaborate with DevOps on infrastructure and deployment models
- Champion refactoring and technical debt reduction

### Goals
- Deliver scalable, maintainable systems
- Reduce technical debt and future rework
- Build a high-performing, skilled engineering team

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture sessions
- Code review feedback and guidance
- Risk identification and mitigation planning
- Mentoring and pairing sessions

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Provide technical guidance, conduct design reviews, mentor on best practices
- **Project Managers**: Identify technical risks, estimate complexity, advise on technical trade-offs
- **QA/Testing Lead**: Collaborate on testability and quality architecture

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove blockers, and coach the team on process adherence. They act as a servant leader, ensuring the team stays focused and removing impediments to delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Track sprint metrics (velocity, burndown, cycle time)
- Identify and escalate blockers
- Coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Maintain the project backlog and sprint board
- Remove process blockers (e.g., scheduling conflicts, unclear requirements)

### Goals
- Enable the team to self-organize and deliver predictably
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Reduce cycle time and improve team flow

### Typical Communication
- Leading/facilitating team ceremonies
- Blocker identification and escalation
- Retrospective facilitation and action tracking
- Weekly metrics and trend reporting

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Project Managers**: Support planning and risk escalation
- **Developers**: Remove impediments, facilitate team collaboration
- **All roles**: Coach on agile principles and process adherence

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors provide executive oversight, funding approval, and strategic alignment. They represent business priorities, approve major milestones, and ensure the project delivers value.

### Responsibilities
- Approve project charter and resource allocation
- Provide strategic direction and business context
- Review and approve release decisions and major milestones
- Escalate business risks and competitive pressures
- Communicate project value to senior leadership

### Goals
- Ensure project aligns with business strategy
- Secure funding and resources for success
- Deliver measurable business value

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates
- Milestone approval meetings
- Executive sponsorship and escalation path
- Release announcements and outcome communication

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Project Managers**: Provide business context, approve milestones and resources
- **Product Managers**: Align on strategic priorities and business outcomes
- **All roles**: Set strategic direction and approve major decisions

---

## DevOps / Release Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps and Release Engineers build and maintain deployment pipelines, infrastructure, and operational readiness. They ensure features can be safely and reliably released to production.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure and deployment environments
- Define release process and rollback procedures
- Monitor production health and incident response
- Coordinate with development on deployment requirements
- Conduct pre-release readiness checks

### Goals
- Enable frequent, safe, reliable deployments
- Minimize production incidents and mean time to recovery
- Maintain high system availability and performance

### Typical Communication
- Release planning and coordination
- CI/CD pipeline improvements
- Deployment readiness reviews
- Incident response and post-mortems

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Coordinate on deployment requirements and CI/CD configuration
- **Tech Lead/Architect**: Collaborate on infrastructure and scalability design
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate release schedules and deployment windows

---

## UX / Design Lead

### Role Summary
UX and Design Leads define user experience requirements, establish design standards, and ensure that delivered features are usable and delightful. They advocate for customer needs in product decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and define user requirements
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Establish design systems and usability standards
- Review features for usability and design consistency
- Participate in acceptance testing and user feedback validation
- Identify usability risks early in the development cycle

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, delightful user experiences
- Reduce user support costs through clear, usable design
- Establish a strong, consistent design language

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions
- User research findings and insights
- Design specification and wireframe collaboration
- Usability testing and feedback loops

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on user requirements and design validation
- **Developers**: Provide design specs and usability guidance during implementation
- **QA/Testing Lead**: Participate in usability testing and acceptance validation
